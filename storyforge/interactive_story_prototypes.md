# Interactive Digital Storytelling Prototypes

## Prototype 1: Fractured Memory

### Theme & Log-line
**Cyberpunk thriller where a data analyst discovers their memories have been systematically altered, and every choice reveals or conceals fragments of their true identity.**

### Core Gameplay/Storytelling Mechanic
**Memory Reconstruction System**: Player choices determine which memories surface or fade. Each decision adds "clarity points" to different memory clusters (Personal, Professional, Romantic, Traumatic). Conflicting memories create cognitive dissonance, forcing players to choose which version of events to believe. The interface displays memory fragments as puzzle pieces that connect or contradict based on choices.

### Main Cast

| Name | Role | Backstory | Motivations | Starting Traits | Character Arc |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Alex Chen** | Protagonist/Data Analyst | Former corporate whistleblower with suppressed memories | Uncover the truth about their identity | Analytical +2, Trust -1, Memory Gaps | Fragmented → Whole or Accepting Uncertainty |
| **Dr. Sarah Voss** | Memory Therapist | Underground neural specialist with hidden agenda | Help Alex while protecting her own secrets | Empathy +3, Deception +2 | Ally → Betrayer or Genuine Helper |
| **Marcus "Glitch" Rivera** | Hacker/Info Broker | Alex's alleged former partner | Profit from information or genuine loyalty | Tech Skills +3, Paranoia +2 | Mercenary → True Friend or Enemy |
| **Director Hayes** | Corporate Antagonist | Head of memory division at MindCorp | Maintain control over memory technology | Authority +3, Ruthless +2 | Villain → Complex Moral Actor |

### Branching Map Overview
- **Decision Point 1**: Trust Dr. Voss's treatment vs. Seek underground hacker help
  - Route A: Medical path → Corporate conspiracy revelation
  - Route B: Street path → Government surveillance discovery
- **Decision Point 2**: Believe recovered romantic memories vs. Question their authenticity  
  - Affects relationship with mysterious "Emma" character
- **Decision Point 3**: Confront memory thief directly vs. Expose them publicly
  - Route A: Personal confrontation → Multiple endings based on trust levels
  - Route B: Public exposure → Systemic change but personal cost
- **Decision Point 4**: Accept reconstructed identity vs. Choose selective amnesia
  - 4 possible endings based on accumulated memory clarity

### Scene Outlines

**Scene 1: The Glitch**
- **Objective**: Introduce memory inconsistency and choice mechanics
- **Setup**: Alex reviewing security footage at work, notices temporal gaps
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Alex discovers 3-hour gap in building surveillance
  - Flashback fragment: arguing with someone (face obscured)
  - Choice: *"Investigate the gap"* vs *"Report to supervisor"* vs *"Ignore and continue work"*
  - Each choice affects Trust (supervisor), Paranoia, or Professional Standing
- **Branch Hook**: Investigating leads to Dr. Voss contact; reporting triggers corporate monitoring
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Alex: "The timestamp jumps from 14:30 to 17:45. That's not a glitch."*
  - *System Message: [Memory fragment surfacing...]*
  - *Voice (distorted): "You can't keep running from what you did."*
  - *Alex: "What I did? I don't remember doing anything."*

**Scene 2: The Underground Session**
- **Objective**: Reveal memory manipulation technology and introduce moral complexity
- **Setup**: Alex meets Dr. Voss in abandoned subway station clinic
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Dr. Voss explains neural editing technology
  - Memory fragment reveals Alex once worked on this same technology
  - Choice: *"I want all memories back"* vs *"Only safe memories"* vs *"I need to think about this"*
  - Glitch appears via hologram, warns about Voss's true identity
- **Branch Hook**: Different choices lead to different memory recovery paths
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Dr. Voss: "Memory isn't just data, Alex. It's identity. Remove the wrong piece..."*
  - *Alex: "And I become someone else entirely."*
  - *Glitch: "Doc's not telling you everything. Ask her about Project Tabula Rasa."*
  - *Dr. Voss: "Marcus, you're out of line."*
  - *Alex: "You two know each other? Another convenient coincidence?"*

**Scene 3: The Choice Chamber**
- **Objective**: Final decision point with full knowledge of consequences
- **Setup**: Alex stands before memory editing machine with all parties present
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Revelation: Alex created the memory editing technology
  - Truth: They voluntarily erased memories after causing accident that killed innocent people
  - Director Hayes offers deal: return to work with modified memories
  - Dr. Voss offers complete restoration including traumatic memories
  - Glitch offers selective editing to create "better" version of truth
- **Branch Hook**: Choice determines ending and character resolution
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Director Hayes: "You can be the hero of your own story, Alex. Just like you wanted."*
  - *Dr. Voss: "Truth isn't comfortable, but it's yours."*
  - *Glitch: "Or we craft something in between. Nobody has to be the villain."*
  - *Alex: "What if I don't want any of these choices?"*
  - *System: "Sometimes the most honest choice is to live with uncertainty."*

### Key Plot Hooks
- **Collectible Memory Fragments**: Hidden memories unlock as players make consistent choices
- **Emma's Messages**: Mysterious texts from alleged former lover, authenticity questionable
- **Corporate Timer**: MindCorp closes in, creating time pressure for decisions
- **Identity Metrics**: Visual representation of personality shifts based on recovered memories
- **The Mirror Scenes**: Periodic moments where Alex sees different reflections based on memory state

## Prototype 2: The Crown's Shadow

### Theme & Log-line  
**Medieval political fantasy where a royal spy must navigate court intrigue through relationship management, with each alliance affecting the kingdom's fate and the player's access to information.**

### Core Gameplay/Storytelling Mechanic
**Relationship Web System**: Player maintains trust, romance, fear, and debt relationships with 8 key court figures. Each conversation choice affects multiple relationship values simultaneously. Higher relationships unlock exclusive information and story branches, but conflicting loyalties create impossible situations where helping one character betrays another.

### Main Cast

| Name | Role | Backstory | Motivations | Starting Traits | Character Arc |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Kira Shadowmere** | Protagonist/Royal Spy | Orphan raised by spymaster, secretly noble-born | Protect the realm while discovering identity | Stealth +3, Loyalty +2, Noble Birth (hidden) | Servant → Ruler or Shadow Master |
| **Prince Aldric** | Heir Apparent | Idealistic but naive, unaware of court corruption | Reform kingdom, prove worthiness | Idealism +3, Naivety +2 | Puppet → True Leader or Corrupted |
| **Lady Morgana Blackthorn** | Court Sorceress | Former rebel, now bound by magical contract | Break her binding, revenge on enemies | Magic +3, Bitter +2, Bound | Antagonist → Ally or Tragic End |
| **Sir Garrett Kane** | Knight Commander | War hero with PTSD, drinks to forget | Maintain honor, protect innocent | Combat +3, Honor +2, Trauma +1 | Broken → Redeemed or Fallen |
| **Merchant Lord Vex** | Trade Master | Self-made wealth, information broker | Expand influence, avoid noble persecution | Wealth +3, Information +3 | Neutral → Kingmaker or Outcast |
| **Queen Regent Isadora** | Ruler | Protecting kingdom until Aldric matures | Maintain power, secure succession | Political +3, Maternal -1, Ruthless +2 | Protective → Controlling or Sacrificial |

### Branching Map Overview
- **Decision Point 1**: Expose corruption to Prince vs. Work within system vs. Gather more evidence
  - Route A: Direct approach → Early confrontation with Queen
  - Route B: Subtle approach → Long-term manipulation game  
  - Route C: Investigation route → Discovery of bigger conspiracy
- **Decision Point 2**: Save Prince from assassination vs. Let attempt proceed for greater good
  - Affects: Prince survival, Queen's trust, rebellion momentum
- **Decision Point 3**: Support magical research vs. Suppress dangerous experiments vs. Steal research for rebels
  - Determines magical ending availability and Lady Morgana's fate
- **Decision Point 4**: Claim royal birthright vs. Support reformed monarchy vs. Enable republic
  - 6 possible endings based on relationship combinations and previous choices

### Scene Outlines

**Scene 1: The Feast of Whispers**
- **Objective**: Establish relationship mechanics and introduce court politics
- **Setup**: Royal feast celebrating peace treaty, but Kira notices poison attempt
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Player circulates among tables, each conversation affecting relationships
  - Discovery: wine intended for Prince is poisoned
  - Choice: *"Warn Prince directly"* vs *"Switch glasses subtly"* vs *"Identify poisoner first"*
  - Each choice affects Trust with different characters and unlocks information
- **Branch Hook**: Different approaches reveal different suspects and motivations
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Lady Morgana: "You move like water through shadows, little spy. But water finds its level."*
  - *Kira: "And shadows follow the light, my lady."*
  - *Prince Aldric: "Sometimes I think the only honest person at court is the one paid to lie."*
  - *Kira: "Honesty is a luxury, Your Highness. Truth is a necessity."*
  - *Sir Garrett: "In war, enemies attack from front. Here, they smile and offer wine."*

**Scene 2: The Sorceress's Bargain**
- **Objective**: Introduce magic system consequences and moral complexity
- **Setup**: Lady Morgana offers magical aid in exchange for future service
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Morgana reveals she knows Kira's true parentage
  - Demonstrates power by showing visions of possible futures
  - Choice: *"Accept magical bond"* vs *"Refuse and maintain independence"* vs *"Attempt to negotiate terms"*
  - Magic users gain power but lose free will; refusing closes magical story branches
- **Branch Hook**: Magical bond affects all future choices and character agency
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Lady Morgana: "Power is never free, child. The question is whether you pay with service or with regret."*
  - *Kira: "And if I refuse?"*
  - *Lady Morgana: "Then you'll face what's coming with only mortal strength. Sometimes that's not enough."*
  - *Kira: "I've survived this long without magic."*
  - *Lady Morgana: "Survival and victory are different games entirely."*

**Scene 3: The Throne Room Trial**
- **Objective**: Final confrontation where all relationships and choices matter
- **Setup**: Prince accused of treason, Kira must testify with evidence gathered
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Each court member speaks based on relationship level with Kira
  - Evidence presentation limited by trust levels and previous choices
  - Queen's judgment affected by her trust in Kira vs. protection of Prince
  - Choice: *"Present all evidence"* vs *"Protect certain allies"* vs *"Reveal own identity to save Prince"*
- **Branch Hook**: Outcome determines kingdom's future and available ending paths
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Queen Isadora: "Speak, shadow. In this light, what truth do you bring?"*
  - *Kira: "Truth is a blade, Your Majesty. It cuts all who touch it."*
  - *Prince Aldric: "I would rather die with honor than live as a puppet."*
  - *Sir Garrett: "Honor without wisdom is just elaborate suicide, boy."*
  - *Merchant Vex: "The crown's value lies not in its gold, but in who pays to place it."*

### Key Plot Hooks
- **The Royal Bloodline**: Genetic markers appear in dreams, affecting magical ability
- **Morgana's Binding**: Visual representation of magical contract tightening with each magical choice
- **The Rebellion Clock**: Underground resistance grows stronger based on court stability
- **Relationship Visualization**: Dynamic court map showing alliance shifts and conflicts
- **The Succession Crisis**: Multiple claimants emerge based on player's earlier choices

## Prototype 3: The Warden's Keep

### Theme & Log-line
**Psychological horror where a night security guard at an abandoned psychiatric hospital must distinguish between supernatural threats and their own deteriorating mental state, with sanity affecting reality perception.**

### Core Gameplay/Storytelling Mechanic
**Sanity Filter System**: Player's mental state affects interface, dialogue options, and story presentation. High sanity reveals logical explanations; low sanity shows supernatural elements. "Stress Events" lower sanity, "Grounding Actions" restore it. Some story branches only accessible at specific sanity levels, creating multiple valid interpretations of events.

### Main Cast

| Name | Role | Backstory | Motivations | Starting Traits | Character Arc |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Jordan Mills** | Protagonist/Security Guard | Former EMT with PTSD, needs money for therapy | Survive the job, maintain sanity | Trauma +2, Practical +2, Sanity: 75% | Stable → Broken or Transcendent |
| **Dr. Elena Vasquez** | Psychiatrist (Recordings) | Former hospital head, disappeared mysteriously | Help patients, hide unethical experiments | Caring +2, Guilt +3 (deceased) | Recorded Voice → Ghostly Guidance or Accusation |
| **Tommy Chen** | Day Shift Guard | Jordan's only human contact, increasingly worried | Protect Jordan, keep job | Friendly +3, Concerned +2 | Supportive → Interventionist or Enabler |
| **Sarah Mills** | Jordan's Sister (Phone) | Trying to help Jordan recover from trauma | Get Jordan proper mental health treatment | Supportive +3, Persistent +2 | Helpful → Frustrated or Understanding |
| **The Patients** | Various Entities | Former residents, unclear if real or imagined | Seek peace, revenge, or acknowledgment | Variable based on sanity level | Threatening → Sympathetic or Vice Versa |

### Branching Map Overview
- **Decision Point 1**: Take medication vs. Stay alert naturally vs. Self-medicate with alcohol
  - Route A: Medicated path → Stable but dulled perception, miss supernatural clues
  - Route B: Natural path → Full experience but vulnerable to breakdown
  - Route C: Alcohol path → Unreliable narrator, unique hallucination content
- **Decision Point 2**: Trust recorded therapy sessions vs. Believe supernatural explanation vs. Seek rational solutions
  - Affects interpretation of all subsequent events
- **Decision Point 3**: Call for help vs. Handle situation alone vs. Leave immediately
  - Route A: Outside intervention → Multiple perspectives on events
  - Route B: Isolation → Full psychological experience
  - Route C: Escape → Consequences follow into outside world
- **Decision Point 4**: Accept supernatural reality vs. Embrace psychological explanation vs. Choose ambiguous truth
  - 5 endings based on sanity level and previous choices

### Scene Outlines

**Scene 1: The First Night**
- **Objective**: Establish sanity mechanics and introduce ambiguous supernatural elements
- **Setup**: Jordan's first patrol through empty hospital wards
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Normal security checks reveal increasing anomalies
  - Discovery: patient room with fresh flowers (impossible)
  - Choice: *"Investigate flowers"* vs *"Report immediately"* vs *"Rationalize and continue"*
  - Each choice affects starting sanity level and available story branches
- **Branch Hook**: Investigation level determines what Jordan discovers about hospital's past
- **Sample Dialogue** (High Sanity):
  - *Jordan: "Someone's been here. The flowers are fresh, water's clean."*
  - *Radio static: "...nobody's been authorized...probably contractors..."*
  - *Jordan: "Right. Contractors. Who leave flowers in patient rooms."*

- **Sample Dialogue** (Low Sanity):
  - *Voice (whispering): "She loved daisies. Before the treatment."*
  - *Jordan: "Who's there? Hello?"*
  - *Voice: "The flowers remember when she doesn't."*
  - *Jordan: "I need to... I need to call Tommy."*

**Scene 2: The Therapy Recordings**
- **Objective**: Reveal hospital's dark past while questioning reliability of information
- **Setup**: Jordan discovers hidden therapy session recordings in basement archives
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Audio reveals experimental treatments on patients
  - Dr. Vasquez's voice shows increasing desperation and guilt
  - Choice: *"Listen to all recordings"* vs *"Stop and report findings"* vs *"Destroy evidence"*
  - Listening lowers sanity but reveals truth; stopping maintains stability but limits knowledge
- **Branch Hook**: Knowledge level affects final confrontation and ending availability
- **Sample Dialogue** (Recording):
  - *Dr. Vasquez: "Patient 23 showed remarkable improvement after the enhanced therapy."*
  - *Unknown Voice: "Define improvement, Doctor."*
  - *Dr. Vasquez: "Complete compliance. No resistance to treatment."*
  - *Jordan (present): "Jesus Christ. What were they doing here?"*

**Scene 3: The Confrontation**
- **Objective**: Final encounter where sanity level determines reality interpretation
- **Setup**: All supernatural/psychological elements converge in former therapy room
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Jordan faces culmination of all previous choices and experiences
  - Reality shifts based on sanity level and player decisions
  - Dr. Vasquez appears (ghost, hallucination, or recording depending on sanity)
  - Choice: *"Confront the truth"* vs *"Deny everything"* vs *"Accept uncertainty"*
- **Branch Hook**: Choice determines reality interpretation and personal resolution
- **Sample Dialogue** (Ambiguous Version):
  - *Dr. Vasquez: "You see them too, don't you? The ones we failed."*
  - *Jordan: "I see... something. I'm not sure what's real anymore."*
  - *Dr. Vasquez: "Real is what helps them find peace. Real is what lets you sleep."*
  - *Jordan: "And if I can't give them peace?"*
  - *Dr. Vasquez: "Then you'll join us in the watching."*

### Key Plot Hooks
- **Sanity Meter**: Visual representation of mental state affecting UI and story options
- **Patient Files**: Collectible records reveal individual stories, affecting supernatural encounters
- **The Medication Timer**: Daily choice to take prescribed medication or face symptoms
- **Reality Glitches**: Interface elements that change based on sanity level
- **The Phone Calls**: Regular check-ins with sister become increasingly strained or supportive

## Prototype 4: Small Town Stories

### Theme & Log-line
**Coming-of-age slice-of-life where a high school senior's daily choices about relationships, academics, and personal values compound over time to determine their post-graduation path and the person they become.**

### Core Gameplay/Storytelling Mechanic
**Ripple Effect System**: Minor daily choices accumulate invisible "life direction points" across categories (Academic, Social, Creative, Family, Romance, Community). Players see immediate small consequences but major life changes emerge gradually. Final outcomes determined by point totals, creating realistic cause-and-effect between small decisions and life direction.

### Main Cast

| Name | Role | Backstory | Motivations | Starting Traits | Character Arc |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Jamie Rivera** | Protagonist/Senior | First-generation college prospect from working family | Balance personal dreams with family expectations | Responsible +2, Ambitious +1, Conflicted +1 | Uncertain → Self-Directed |
| **Ms. Patricia Chen** | English Teacher | Former journalist turned educator, believes in Jamie | Help Jamie reach potential, relive own dreams | Inspiring +3, Persistent +2 | Mentor → Friend or Disappointed |
| **Alex Thompson** | Best Friend | Class clown hiding family problems | Maintain friendships, avoid serious conversations | Humorous +3, Avoidant +2 | Surface → Deep or Distant |
| **Sam Garcia** | Romantic Interest | Academic rival, secretly supportive | Academic success, understand Jamie | Competitive +2, Caring +2 | Rival → Partner or Separate Paths |
| **Maria Rivera** | Younger Sister | Looks up to Jamie, has different dreams | Gain independence, support family | Idealistic +2, Supportive +3 | Dependent → Independent |
| **Carlos Rivera** | Father | Works two jobs, wants security for children | Provide for family, see children succeed safely | Protective +3, Traditional +2 | Strict → Understanding or Disappointed |

### Branching Map Overview
- **Decision Point 1**: Focus on grades vs. Part-time job vs. Balance both
  - Route A: Academic focus → College scholarships but family stress
  - Route B: Work focus → Financial help but missed opportunities
  - Route C: Balance → Moderate success in both areas
- **Decision Point 2**: Maintain hometown relationships vs. Pursue distant opportunities vs. Try to have both
  - Affects final relationship states and geographical endings
- **Decision Point 3**: Follow family expectations vs. Pursue personal passion vs. Find compromise
  - Route A: Safe path → Security but personal unfulfillment questions
  - Route B: Risk path → Passion pursuit with uncertain outcomes
  - Route C: Middle ground → Complex but potentially satisfying balance
- **Decision Point 4**: Stay local vs. Move away vs. Return home plan
  - 8 possible life direction endings based on accumulated choices

### Scene Outlines

**Scene 1: The College Fair**
- **Objective**: Introduce choice consequences and family dynamics
- **Setup**: Jamie attends college fair with conflicting family expectations
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Father wants Jamie to focus on local, practical options
  - Ms. Chen encourages applications to prestigious schools
  - Sam suggests compromise: competitive programs at state schools
  - Choice: *"Focus on safety schools"* vs *"Apply to reach schools"* vs *"Mix of both"*
  - Each choice affects family stress and opportunity levels
- **Branch Hook**: Application choices determine later financial aid and acceptance scenes
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Carlos: "Community college is good education. And you can live at home, help with Maria."*
  - *Ms. Chen: "Jamie has the potential for any school in the country."*
  - *Jamie: "What if I fail? What if I get in somewhere I can't afford?"*
  - *Sam: "What if you don't try and spend your life wondering?"*
  - *Maria: "I think you should do what makes you happy."*

**Scene 2: The Part-Time Job Decision**
- **Objective**: Show immediate vs. long-term consequence trade-offs
- **Setup**: Jamie offered job at local bookstore during crucial study period
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Family needs extra income for household expenses
  - Job would interfere with study time and extracurricular activities
  - Alex warns about missing out on senior year experiences
  - Choice: *"Take the job"* vs *"Focus on school"* vs *"Negotiate limited hours"*
  - Decision affects family relationship, academic performance, and social life
- **Branch Hook**: Work experience opens different summer opportunities but affects grades
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Bookstore Owner: "You'd be perfect here. Smart, reliable, knows books."*
  - *Jamie: "I want to help, but I'm worried about my grades."*
  - *Alex: "Senior year only happens once. You'll regret missing everything for work."*
  - *Carlos: "Work teaches responsibility. School teaches thinking. You need both."*
  - *Jamie: "Why can't anything be simple?"*

**Scene 3: The Graduation Speech**
- **Objective**: Culminate character growth and reveal life direction
- **Setup**: Jamie chosen to give valedictorian speech, must decide what message to share
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Speech content reflects accumulated choices and values
  - Audience includes all important characters whose relationships vary based on previous decisions
  - Final choice: *"Inspire others to take risks"* vs *"Advocate for practical wisdom"* vs *"Embrace uncertainty as growth"*
  - Speech determines final reputation and impacts other characters' futures
- **Branch Hook**: Speech choice affects epilogue showing long-term impact on community
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Jamie: "We've been told to follow our dreams. But what if our dreams change?"*
  - *Audience Member: "What if they don't come true?"*
  - *Jamie: "Then we learn that dreams aren't destinations. They're directions."*
  - *Ms. Chen (thinking): "I knew they had it in them."*
  - *Carlos (thinking): "My child is going to be okay."*

### Key Plot Hooks
- **The Relationship Web**: Visual map showing how choices affect multiple relationships simultaneously
- **The Dream Journal**: Player tracks Jamie's changing aspirations based on experiences
- **College Application Tracker**: Stress-inducing countdown with acceptance/rejection consequences
- **The Family Dinner**: Regular scenes where choices affect household dynamics
- **Senior Year Timeline**: Major events (prom, graduation, summer plans) influenced by accumulated decisions
- **The Five-Year Forward**: Epilogue scenes showing long-term consequences of seemingly minor choices

## Prototype 5: The Last Convoy

### Theme & Log-line
**Post-apocalyptic survival drama where the leader of a refugee convoy must balance resource management with moral choices, as each decision affects group dynamics, individual character fates, and the convoy's chances of reaching safety.**

### Core Gameplay/Storytelling Mechanic
**Resource vs. Morality Engine**: Player manages five resources (Food, Fuel, Medicine, Morale, Trust) while making ethical decisions. Harsh choices preserve resources but lower morale and trust; compassionate choices drain resources but maintain group cohesion. Different character types respond differently to player's leadership style, creating complex group dynamics.

### Main Cast

| Name | Role | Backstory | Motivations | Starting Traits | Character Arc |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Captain Ray Santos** | Protagonist/Convoy Leader | Former military logistics officer, reluctant leader | Get everyone to safety, maintain humanity | Leadership +3, Guilt +1, Tactical +2 | Burden → Acceptance or Breakdown |
| **Dr. Amara Singh** | Medic | Surgeon who lost family, dedicated to saving lives | Preserve medical resources, heal trauma | Medical +3, Compassionate +3, Grief +2 | Healer → Hardened or Martyred |
| **Marcus "Tank" Rodriguez** | Security Chief | Ex-cop, believes in harsh discipline | Maintain order, protect convoy by any means | Combat +3, Authoritarian +2, Loyal +2 | Enforcer → Understanding or Tyrant |
| **Elena Petrov** | Supply Manager | Former teacher, reluctant organizer | Keep group together, protect the children | Organized +2, Protective +3, Diplomatic +1 | Peacekeeper → Leader or Overwhelmed |
| **Old Pete** | Scout/Mechanic | Drifter who knows the wasteland | Survive, find purpose in helping others | Survival +3, Independent +2, Wise +1 | Loner → Family or Sacrifice |
| **Lisa Wong** | Teenager | Lost parents, refuses to show weakness | Prove worth, find belonging | Stubborn +2, Brave +2, Traumatized +1 | Angry → Mature or Broken |

### Branching Map Overview
- **Decision Point 1**: Accept more refugees vs. Maintain manageable group size vs. Screen carefully
  - Route A: Humanitarian path → Resource strain but moral authority
  - Route B: Pragmatic path → Stability but guilt and reputation damage
  - Route C: Selective path → Balanced but creates internal moral conflicts
- **Decision Point 2**: Share resources equally vs. Prioritize contributors vs. Protect the vulnerable
  - Affects group unity and individual character loyalty
- **Decision Point 3**: Take dangerous shortcut vs. Safe but resource-draining route vs. Split the convoy
  - Route A: Risk path → Quick progress but potential disasters
  - Route B: Careful path → Safety but starvation risk
  - Route C: Split decision → Parallel storylines with different outcomes
- **Decision Point 4**: Settle in found sanctuary vs. Continue to original destination vs. Establish own community
  - 6 possible endings based on group composition, resources, and trust levels

### Scene Outlines

**Scene 1: The Roadblock Decision**
- **Objective**: Introduce resource management and moral choice mechanics
- **Setup**: Convoy encounters armed survivors blocking bridge, demanding "toll"
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Bandits want half the convoy's food for safe passage
  - Alternative routes cost fuel and time
  - Some convoy members advocate fighting
  - Choice: *"Pay the toll"* vs *"Fight through"* vs *"Find alternate route"*
  - Each choice costs different resources and affects character relationships
- **Branch Hook**: Reputation with other survivor groups affected by choice
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Bandit Leader: "Nice convoy. Be a shame if something happened to it."*
  - *Tank: "Say the word, Captain. We can take them."*
  - *Dr. Singh: "We can't risk the children in a firefight."*
  - *Santos: "Every choice costs us something. What can we afford to lose?"*
  - *Elena: "If we give in now, every group on the road will see us as easy targets."*

**Scene 2: The Medicine Crisis**
- **Objective**: Force difficult resource allocation decisions with personal stakes
- **Setup**: Flu outbreak hits convoy; limited medicine forces choice of who to treat
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Dr. Singh explains medical supplies only sufficient for half the sick
  - Different characters argue for different priority systems
  - Lisa becomes critically ill, testing player's personal attachments
  - Choice: *"Treat children first"* vs *"Treat essential personnel"* vs *"Random selection"*
  - Decision affects group morale, character survival, and future medical options
- **Branch Hook**: Survivors remember who was prioritized; affects later loyalty
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Dr. Singh: "I became a doctor to save lives. Now I have to choose who dies."*
  - *Old Pete: "In the old world, we'd treat everyone. This ain't that world."*
  - *Elena: "How do we look at the children we don't save?"*
  - *Lisa: "Don't... don't waste medicine on me. I'm tough enough."*
  - *Santos: "Tough isn't enough against this disease, kid."*

**Scene 3: The Safe Haven**
- **Objective**: Final decision determining convoy's ultimate fate
- **Setup**: Convoy reaches fortified settlement that offers safety with conditions
- **Beat-by-beat**:
  - Settlement leader explains entry requirements and rules
  - Some convoy members want to stay; others want to continue
  - Player's past decisions affect negotiating position and group unity
  - Choice: *"Accept settlement terms"* vs *"Negotiate better deal"* vs *"Leave together"*
  - Final choice determines ending and character fates
- **Branch Hook**: Group splits or stays together based on accumulated trust and leadership decisions
- **Sample Dialogue**:
  - *Settlement Leader: "We have room for useful people. Others... we'll consider case by case."*
  - *Tank: "Useful? These people trusted us to keep them together."*
  - *Dr. Singh: "Some of our injured won't survive if we keep traveling."*
  - *Elena: "We've come this far as a family. Do we abandon that now?"*
  - *Santos: "I promised to get you to safety. I didn't promise what that would cost."*
  - *Old Pete: "Sometimes the journey matters more than the destination."*

### Key Plot Hooks
- **Resource Dashboard**: Visual tracking of supplies with consequences for depletion
- **Character Loyalty Meter**: Individual relationships affect group dynamics and ending availability
- **The Radio Chatter**: Information about other survivor groups and potential destinations
- **Convoy Status Board**: Visual representation of vehicle condition, passenger health, and group morale
- **The Leadership Journal**: Player's decisions recorded, affecting reputation with encountered groups
- **Weather and Hazard System**: Environmental challenges that test resource management and leadership decisions
- **The Memorial Wall**: Tribute to convoy members lost, affecting group morale and player emotional investment

## Summary

These five prototypes offer distinct interactive storytelling experiences across different genres, each with unique mechanics that support their thematic goals:

1. **Fractured Memory** uses psychological choice mechanics to explore identity and truth
2. **Crown's Shadow** employs relationship management to navigate political intrigue
3. **Warden's Keep** leverages sanity mechanics to blur reality and perception
4. **Small Town Stories** applies accumulative choice consequences to coming-of-age themes
